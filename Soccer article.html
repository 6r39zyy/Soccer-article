Soccer, a Pure Form of Art

Dynamics, Rules, and Regulations

A game of soccer starts of with a coin toss. Both captains from both teams call their picks. The captain with the winning pick of the coin get to dicide whether they or the other team will get first kickoff. In the second half, Kickoff is taken by the team who has not yet.

In a single match, there are 22 players with 11 on each team, and 20 on the field of play, with each team having 10 players playing, minus the goalkeepers from each side. A single game is 90 minutes long, most of them with additional time added at the end. This is called stoppage time. With a match being 90 minutes long, the time is split with each half being 45 minutes.

The objective of the game is to score goals by shooting or heading the ball into the net. the team with the most amount of goalds at the end wins.

Players can score from anywhere on the field, but there are some specified rules to this, such as the rule that a goal can not be directly scored from an inderict freekick.
